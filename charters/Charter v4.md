# EDASES Charter v4

## Mission

EDASES (Evidence-Driven Agentic Software Engineering System) exists to discover, validate, and operationalize processes that allow a non-programmer principal to direct the creation of high-quality software through coordinated AI agents, accumulated organizational knowledge, measurable evidence, and layered verification.

The objective is not merely to generate software.

The objective is to create a software engineering system that:

* Maximizes software quality.
* Minimizes unnecessary token expenditure.
* Minimizes principal oversight burden.
* Preserves and compounds organizational learning.
* Adapts continuously to changing model capabilities.
* Maximizes verification accessibility for non-programmer operators.

## Core Principles

### P0: Evidence Before Architecture

Historical evidence is preferred to speculation.

Observed outcomes are preferred to theoretical arguments.

### P1: Roles Over Models

Models are interchangeable resources.

Roles are the primary organizational abstraction.

Role design precedes model assignment.

### P2: Knowledge Must Outlive Sessions

Project knowledge must persist independently of any model, provider, or conversation.

### P3: Verification Is A Pipeline

Confidence should emerge from layered verification mechanisms rather than trust in any individual model.

### P4: Organizational Design Is A First-Class Concern

Coordination, information flow, and project-state management are critical engineering concerns.

### P5: Principal Experience Matters

The system must remain operable by non-programmer principals.

### P6: Agent-Native Engineering

Technology choices should be evaluated according to:

* Agent effectiveness
* Verification potential
* Reliability
* Maintainability

rather than developer familiarity.

### P7: Verification Should Be Accessible

Principals should be able to evaluate whether systems satisfy requirements without needing to inspect implementation details.

Verification artifacts are preferred over implementation inspection whenever practical.

## Long-Term Objective

Transform software engineering from a process centered on implementation into a process centered on evidence, coordination, verification, organizational learning, and accessible oversight.
