# EDASES Operational Testbed Charter

## Purpose

This document defines the operational testbed through which EDASES will gather evidence, evaluate assumptions, and observe the practical application of AI-assisted software engineering practices.

The purpose of the testbed is not to validate predetermined conclusions. Its purpose is to provide a real operational environment in which assumptions, practices, workflows, and organizational structures can be evaluated against observable outcomes.

The current operational testbed is the Hospitality Management Suite project.

---

# Testbed Overview

The Hospitality Management Suite is a software platform being developed to support real-world hospitality operations, initially within a multi-location hostel environment.

The project is intended to address operational challenges currently distributed across multiple independent systems and workflows.

Unlike controlled experiments or synthetic development exercises, the Hospitality Management Suite operates within an active business environment involving:

* Real users
* Real operational workflows
* Real operational constraints
* Real consequences for success and failure
* Long-term system usage
* Continuous feedback cycles

This environment provides opportunities for observation that are difficult to obtain through isolated software projects or theoretical exercises.

---

# Rationale For Selection

The Hospitality Management Suite was selected as the primary EDASES operational testbed because it possesses several characteristics valuable to research.

## Embedded Observation

The principal investigator is directly involved in the operational environment being studied.

This enables:

* Direct observation of workflows
* Continuous feedback collection
* Rapid validation of assumptions
* Immediate identification of operational pain points

The ability to observe both system use and organizational behavior provides a richer source of evidence than post-hoc reporting alone.

---

## Operational Relevance

The software addresses active operational problems.

Development decisions therefore have practical consequences and must be evaluated according to operational outcomes rather than theoretical merit.

This creates natural pressure toward useful solutions and discourages purely speculative development.

---

## Incremental Deployment

The project can be deployed incrementally.

Capabilities may be introduced individually, evaluated through operational use, and revised before broader adoption.

This allows assumptions and practices to be evaluated continuously throughout development rather than only after project completion.

---

## Longitudinal Observation

The project is expected to remain in active use over an extended period.

This enables observation of:

* Adoption patterns
* Workflow evolution
* Knowledge retention
* Organizational adaptation
* System maintenance
* Long-term software quality

Many software development studies focus primarily on initial implementation. The testbed provides an opportunity to observe longer-term outcomes.

---

## Multi-Domain Operations

Hospitality operations involve multiple overlapping domains, including:

* Scheduling
* Communications
* Documentation
* Financial tracking
* Resource allocation
* Staff coordination
* Volunteer management
* Reservations

This diversity creates opportunities to observe how development practices perform across different categories of operational problems within a single environment.

# Research Opportunities

The testbed provides opportunities to investigate a variety of questions relevant to EDASES.

Examples include:

## Knowledge Management

* How should project knowledge be structured?
* What information should be externalized?
* How does documentation affect outcomes?

## Verification

* What forms of verification are most effective?
* How much verification is required?
* Which verification approaches are understandable to non-specialists?

## Role Specialization

* Which responsibilities naturally separate?
* Which forms of specialization improve outcomes?
* How should responsibilities be distributed between humans and AI systems?

## Agent Coordination

* How should implementation work be decomposed?
* What orchestration structures emerge naturally?
* Which orchestration structures remain effective as complexity increases?

## Human Oversight

* Which decisions benefit most from human involvement?
* Which responsibilities can be delegated safely?
* Where do human bottlenecks emerge?

## Operational Adoption

* Which software characteristics promote adoption?
* Which characteristics create resistance?
* How do workflows evolve following deployment?

# Constraints And Threats To Validity

The testbed possesses several limitations that must be considered when evaluating findings.

## Embedded Principal

The principal investigator possesses substantial familiarity with the operational environment.

While this provides valuable context, it may also influence observations, decisions, and interpretations.

Findings should therefore be evaluated carefully before being generalized.

---

## Single Organization

Initial observations originate from a single organization operating within a specific hospitality context.

Additional environments may be required before broad conclusions can be justified.

---

## Domain Specificity

Hospitality operations may exhibit characteristics not present in other domains.

Observed outcomes should not automatically be assumed to transfer to unrelated environments.

---

## Evolving Methodology

EDASES remains an active research program.

The processes being evaluated may themselves change throughout the lifetime of the testbed.

As a result, findings should be interpreted within the context of the methodology in use at the time they were generated.

---

# Relationship Between Product And Research

The Hospitality Management Suite and EDASES are separate but related initiatives.

The Hospitality Management Suite exists to solve operational problems.

EDASES exists to investigate AI-assisted software engineering practices.

The Hospitality Management Suite generates evidence relevant to EDASES.

EDASES may influence planning, documentation, verification, and implementation practices applied within the Hospitality Management Suite.

Research influence should be documented through the Assumption-to-Decision Register and related artifacts.

---

# Operational Boundaries

The needs of the operational environment take precedence over research objectives.

Research activities shall not justify:

* Reduced software quality
* Increased operational risk
* Excessive process overhead
* Deliberate disruption of operations

The primary measure of success for the Hospitality Management Suite remains operational usefulness.

Research activities should complement that objective rather than compete with it.

---

# Success Criteria

The operational testbed shall be considered successful if it generates sufficient evidence to evaluate meaningful EDASES assumptions while simultaneously delivering useful operational software.

Indicators of success include:

* Active software adoption
* Sustained operational use
* Accumulation of evaluable evidence
* Identification of validated assumptions
* Identification of invalid assumptions
* Emergence of new research questions

Success does not require assumptions to be confirmed.

Success requires assumptions to become observable and evaluable.

---

# Charter Review

This charter should be reviewed periodically as both the Hospitality Management Suite and EDASES evolve.

Updates should reflect:

* New evidence
* Expanded operational environments
* Additional testbeds
* Revised research priorities
* Changes to methodology

The charter is intended to remain a living document throughout the lifetime of the research program.
