# Phase 1: Knowledge Architecture Research

## Purpose

This project is intended to support a long-term research program that will generate and synthesize information from a variety of sources over time.

Before substantial research can be conducted, a suitable knowledge architecture must be established. The purpose of this phase is to investigate how research knowledge can be structured, stored, connected, and retrieved in a way that remains useful as the volume and complexity of information increases.

The objective is not to design a final system. The objective is to understand the available approaches, identify useful patterns, and establish an initial structure that can evolve alongside the research itself.

---

## Goals

### Goal 1: Identify Existing Approaches

Survey existing systems used for organizing complex bodies of knowledge, including but not limited to:

* Research repositories
* Digital gardens
* Knowledge graphs
* Wikis
* Documentation systems
* Intelligence analysis systems
* Academic citation networks
* Agent-oriented knowledge systems

The intention is to understand how different communities approach the problem of organizing interconnected information.

---

### Goal 2: Identify Core Knowledge Objects

Determine the types of information that commonly appear within research programs.

Examples may include:

* Observations
* Sources
* Assumptions
* Questions
* Findings
* Experiments
* Decisions

This list should remain flexible and may change as research progresses.

---

### Goal 3: Identify Relationship Patterns

Investigate how knowledge systems represent relationships between information.

Examples may include:

* Supports
* Contradicts
* Derived from
* References
* Influences
* Supersedes

The focus should be on understanding how relationships contribute to navigation, synthesis, and discovery.

---

### Goal 4: Evaluate Human and Agent Accessibility

Assess how different approaches support:

* Human navigation and understanding
* Agent-assisted retrieval and analysis
* Long-term maintainability
* Knowledge reuse

The research should consider both human and machine consumers of the information.

---

### Goal 5: Establish an Initial Knowledge Structure

Develop an initial organizational model suitable for the project's current needs.

This structure should be considered provisional and expected to evolve as new requirements emerge.

---

## Research Questions

The following questions are intended as starting points rather than fixed requirements:

* How do successful knowledge systems organize concepts and relationships?
* What information structures scale effectively over time?
* How is provenance tracked and preserved?
* How are revisions and evolving understanding represented?
* How can knowledge remain accessible without becoming rigid?
* What approaches support both human understanding and automated processing?

Additional questions may be introduced throughout the phase.

---

## Methods

Potential methods include:

* Literature review
* Analysis of existing open-source projects
* Examination of public research repositories
* Comparative evaluation of knowledge systems
* Prototype implementations
* Small-scale experiments

The specific methods used should be determined by the evidence encountered during the research process.

---

## Deliverables

The exact deliverables will depend on the findings of the research, but may include:

### Knowledge Architecture Survey

A review of existing approaches and relevant systems.

### Pattern Catalog

A collection of recurring structures, concepts, and relationship models identified during the research.

### Initial Knowledge Model

A proposed structure for organizing project knowledge.

### Evaluation Criteria

A set of criteria that can be used to assess future tools, systems, and approaches.

### Recommendations

Recommendations for the next stage of implementation and experimentation.

---

## Success Criteria

This phase should be considered successful if it produces:

* A clearer understanding of available knowledge architecture approaches
* An initial structure suitable for organizing ongoing research
* A documented rationale for architectural decisions
* A foundation that can evolve as the research program expands

The primary outcome is not a finished system, but an informed starting point for future development.
