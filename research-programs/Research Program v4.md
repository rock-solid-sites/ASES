# EDASES Research Program v4

## Objective

Convert operational experience into evidence.

Convert evidence into validated findings.

Convert validated findings into reusable engineering practices.

## Phase 0: Historical Evidence Extraction

Priority: Highest

EDASES begins with a substantial body of operational experience.

The first objective is to systematically extract evidence from existing projects before conducting new experiments.

### Primary Evidence Sources

* Session notes
* Git-versioned retrospectives
* Claude chat histories
* CLAUDE.md revisions
* Crosslink databases
* Handoff documents
* Architecture decisions
* Repository histories

### Key Deliverables

#### Evidence Register

Catalog observed outcomes and supporting evidence.

#### Workflow Evolution Timeline

Reconstruct the progression from:

Chat Sessions
→ Documentation
→ Handoffs
→ Retrospectives
→ Structured Tracking
→ Crosslink
→ Role Specialization
→ Multi-Agent Systems
→ Multi-Model Systems

#### Organizational Archaeology

Identify naturally emerging:

* Roles
* Information flows
* Coordination structures
* Failure modes
* Success patterns

## Phase 1: Assumption Classification

Classify assumptions as:

* Verified
* Strong Evidence
* Tested
* Plausible
* Unknown

## Phase 2: Targeted Validation

Priority areas:

1. Organizational Intelligence
2. Verification
3. Capability Intelligence
4. Agent-Native Engineering
5. Principal Experience

## Phase 3: Continuous Evidence Collection

Every completed project becomes part of the evidence corpus.

All findings update:

* Evidence Register
* Capability Registry
* Technology Registry
* Organizational Registry
* Verification Registry

## Success Criteria

### Process Success

Major decisions are increasingly informed by evidence rather than intuition.

### Outcome Success

The system demonstrates measurable improvement in:

* Software quality
* Verification coverage
* Principal oversight efficiency
* Token efficiency
* Knowledge reuse
* Project success rates
