# EDASES Operational Research Program

## Phase 0: Evidence Extraction & Testbed Definition

### Purpose

This document defines the current state, objectives, assumptions, research questions, and operational testbed of the EDASES research program.

It does not define a final methodology.

It defines the current research effort and the environment through which evidence will be gathered.

---

# Current Program State

EDASES is currently in Phase 0.

The primary objective of Phase 0 is the extraction, organization, and evaluation of evidence from historical and ongoing AI-assisted software engineering projects.

The goal is to identify recurring patterns, assumptions, failure modes, and successful practices before attempting to define a prescriptive methodology.

Current outputs include:

* Assumption Register
* Research Handoffs
* Research Addenda
* Project Retrospectives
* Architecture Reviews
* Historical Project Analysis

The program remains exploratory.

Many assumptions remain unverified.

---

# Research Objective

The central objective of EDASES is to investigate whether a structured, evidence-driven approach to AI-assisted software engineering can produce more reliable outcomes than ad hoc development practices.

Particular attention is given to:

* Knowledge management
* Organizational design
* Role specialization
* Verification
* Capability routing
* Information flow
* Principal oversight

---

# Research Tracks

The research program currently consists of three parallel tracks.

## Track A: Historical Evidence Extraction

Purpose:

Review completed and ongoing projects to identify recurring patterns and derive candidate assumptions.

Questions include:

* What practices emerged naturally?
* What contributed to successful outcomes?
* What failure modes were observed?
* Which assumptions are supported by evidence?

Outputs:

* Evidence Register
* Assumption Validation
* Workflow Evolution Analysis

---

## Track B: External Research

Purpose:

Investigate current state-of-the-art methodologies, tools, harnesses, frameworks, and organizational approaches used by other practitioners.

Questions include:

* What approaches are currently in use?
* What evidence supports them?
* Which capabilities are already solved?
* Which problems remain unresolved?

Outputs:

* Harness Evaluations
* Methodology Reviews
* Capability Mapping

---

## Track C: Operational Testbed

Purpose:

Generate new evidence through the development and deployment of a real software system used by real operators in a real operational environment.

Current Testbed:

Hospitality Management Suite

Characteristics:

* Active operational environment
* Real users
* Direct observation
* Continuous feedback
* Production consequences
* Longitudinal evaluation

The Hospitality Management Suite exists primarily as a software product.

Its value to EDASES is that it provides an environment in which assumptions can be evaluated against operational reality.

---

# Research Assumptions

Current assumptions are maintained separately within the Assumption Register.

Assumptions should be treated as hypotheses unless supported by evidence.

Research activities should prioritize validation, refinement, or rejection of assumptions.

---

# Research Feedback Loop

The relationship between EDASES and the Hospitality Management Suite is bidirectional.

Hospitality Suite
→ generates evidence for EDASES

EDASES
→ informs planning, verification, documentation, and implementation practices within the Hospitality Suite

Research findings may influence project decisions.

Such influences should be documented to enable future evaluation.

---

# Open Questions

Examples include:

* What role structures emerge naturally?
* Which role structures are optimal?
* What information flows best support multi-agent systems?
* How much verification is necessary?
* What knowledge structures produce the best outcomes?
* Which responsibilities can be delegated safely?
* Which decisions require human oversight?
* Can findings be transferred to other projects and operators?

---

# Success Criteria

Phase 0 will be considered successful when:

1. Historical evidence has been extracted and organized.
2. Core assumptions have been documented.
3. Major research questions have been identified.
4. The operational testbed is established.
5. Initial evidence from the testbed has begun to accumulate.

Only after completion of these objectives should the program attempt to formalize a generalized methodology.
