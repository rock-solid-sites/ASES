# EDASES Research Program v3

## Objective

Convert operational experience into evidence.

Convert evidence into validated findings.

Convert validated findings into reusable engineering practices.

---

# Phase 0: Historical Evidence Extraction

Priority: Highest

EDASES begins with an existing body of operational experience.

Before conducting new experiments, historical project data should be analyzed and incorporated into the evidence base.

---

## Evidence Sources

Primary Sources:

* Session notes
* Git-versioned retrospectives
* Claude chat histories
* CLAUDE.md revisions
* Crosslink databases
* Project conventions
* Repository histories
* Handoff documents
* Architecture decision records

Secondary Sources:

* External documentation
* Industry practices
* Academic research
* Public agent workflows

---

## Outputs

### Evidence Register

Catalog observed outcomes, including:

* architecture pivots,
* workflow evolution,
* model specialization,
* framework selection,
* verification outcomes,
* project delivery outcomes.

Chat-Based Development
→ Documentation

---

### Organizational Archaeology

Identify recurring:

* roles,
* information flows,
* coordination structures,
* failure modes,
* successful patterns.

This work directly informs the Organizational Intelligence research track.

---

# Phase 1: Assumption Classification

Classify assumptions as:

* Verified
* Strong Evidence
* Tested
* Plausible
* Unknown

Promote assumptions when sufficient evidence exists.

---

# Phase 2: Targeted Validation

Only assumptions with meaningful uncertainty require dedicated experimentation.

Priority Areas:

1. Organizational Intelligence
2. Verification
3. Capability Intelligence
4. Agent-Native Engineering
5. Principal Experience

---

# Phase 3: Continuous Evidence Collection

Every completed project becomes part of the research corpus.

New evidence updates:

* Evidence Register
* Capability Registry
* Technology Registry
* Organizational Registry
* Verification Registry

---

## Success Criteria

### Process Success

Major design decisions are increasingly informed by accumulated evidence rather than intuition.

### Outcome Success

Over time, the system demonstrates measurable improvement in:

* software quality,
* verification coverage,
* principal oversight efficiency,
* token efficiency,
* knowledge reuse,
* project delivery success.
